Background

Why this program exists

People at AI policy organizations in DC keep telling us the same thing: they struggle to hire people who combine a solid understanding of AI safety with a basic grasp of the technology itself. At the same time, the AI safety pipeline produces far more junior technical talent than there are research roles, and the upcoming midterm elections may open up many positions in congressional offices.

Looking at the existing routes into AI policy, they either ask for a long commitment, run online, or focus on policy research. There was no short, high-signal way for someone who is curious but uncommitted to explore the whole breadth of policy careers, especially the roles that are not research. That is the gap this program fills.

We would count the first cohort a success if it helps a third of the participants take real next steps toward a policy career. And for everyone else, two weeks of understanding how DC works makes their technical or generalist work better too.
